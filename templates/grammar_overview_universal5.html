Future Tense

Perfective Verbs

The Czech language distinguish 2 verbal aspects. Perfective and imperfective. Perfective verbs express that an action has been completed and they can be only used when referring to future or past, not present.
They are formed by prefixes, the most common of which are: -u, -za, -na, -vy, -po, -pře

* dělám (I do) x udělám (I will do)
* vařím (I cook) x uvařím (I will do)
* telefonuje (He calls) x zatelefonuje (He will call)
* jedu (I go) x pojedu (I will go)
* učím se (I learn) x naučím se (I will learn)
* čteme (We read) x přečteme (We will read)
* peru (I do the washing) x vyperu (I will do the washing)

Most Czech verbs have a perfective and imperfective form. There is no rule that would determine which prefix has to be used for a particular verb, so when learning a verb it is necessary to memorize both the imperfective form and its perfective counterpart.

Imperfective Verbs

Imperfective verbs express an uncompleted or repeated action and can be used when referring to past, present and future.
The future tense in these verbs is formed by an auxiliary verb and infinitive:

Jábududělat Mybudemedělat
Tybudeš dělat Vybudetedělat
Onbudedělat Onibudoudělat
