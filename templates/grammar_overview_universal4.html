Past Participle

The past participle is formed by dropping the final -t in of an infinitive form and replacing it with -l

pracovat x pracoval

Agreement

The past participle has to agree with its subject in gender and number

Ona pracovala
Ony pracovaly
On pracoval
Oni pracovali

Irregular Participles

Some participles are irregular and have to be memorized:

- říct - řekl
- číst - četl
- pomoct - pomohl
- jít - šel/šla
- jíst - jedl
- péct - pekl
- utéct - utekl

Word Order

In Czech personal pronouns are not obligatory since the person is already expressed by the verb ending. In case the personal pronoun is missing, the past participle will precede the auxiliary verb "být".

Pracoval jsem.

While if the subject is explicitly expressed by a pronoun or a noun, the past particile will follow the auxiliary verb.

Já jsem pracoval.
